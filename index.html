RAD-01Overview

What happens to life when you remove radiation entirely, and what does that tell us about surviving in space? That question sits at the centre of my work. I study how organisms respond to the extreme ends of the radiation spectrum: from the sub-background silence of deep underground laboratories like SNOLAB, to the high-flux environments of low Earth orbit.

As a PhD researcher in Radiation Biology and CTO of a stealth-mode space biotech startup, I bridge fundamental biological discovery with applied space engineering, building the tools, systems, and knowledge base needed for long-duration human presence beyond Earth.

Currently

  • Coordinating the GUY project, running centralised omics analysis on samples from international deep underground laboratories to assess global replicability of low-background radiation effects; also piloting automated continuous culture systems at SNOLAB.
  • Giving a talk at the Northern Health Research Conference, Sudbury, June 2026.
  • Co-authoring a space hardware database paper with the NASA OSDR Hardware Analysis Working Group.
