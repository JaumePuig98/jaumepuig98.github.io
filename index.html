About Me

I am a space biotechnology researcher with a foundation in biomedical engineering and a passion for improving life in extreme environments. My work focuses on pioneering research into radiation tolerance and the human microbiome, bridging astrophysics and synthetic biology to address the challenges of long-term space exploration while enhancing health on Earth. From laboratory experiments to international collaborations, I thrive at the intersection of innovation and discovery.

As a strong advocate for open science, I actively contribute to global initiatives like NASA GeneLab and the iGEM Space Network, driving advancements in space biology and multiomics. I believe that transparency and collaboration are key to unlocking the full potential of scientific progress for humanity's benefit.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to STEAM education, inspiring the next generation through programs like iGEM, AulaSPUTNIK, and Lecturers Without Borders. Through mentorships, lectures, and interactive workshops, I aim to connect complex scientific concepts with real-world applications, making science engaging and accessible to learners of all ages.

Research Projects

Skin Microbiome and Radiation Resilience

Universitat Pompeu Fabra & Laboratorio Subterráneo de Canfranc 
2022 - Present

Exploring DNA repair and radiation tolerance in the skin microbiome using transcriptomics across diverse radiation environments, including low-background conditions.
Learn more about the Laboratorio Subterráneo de Canfranc

Pharmacomicrobiomics and Space Medicine

BioAstra 
2024 - Present

Interpreting pharmacology and genomic data from space missions to advance research in the commercial astronaut sector, with a focus on pharmacogenomics and space omics.
Learn more about BioAstra

DNA Damage Protection in LEO

Universitat Pompeu Fabra & Hospital del Mar 
2020 - 2021

Engineering bacteria with DNA repair genes from radio-tolerant species to study and enhance survival under simulated Low Earth Orbit conditions.
Read more in our article in Frotiers in Microbiology

Intein-Mediated Biosensors for Thyroid Hormones

Universitat Pompeu Fabra
2020 - 2024

Engineering an optimized bacterial biosensor to measure thyroid hormone levels in real time, providing a foundation for hormone therapy advancements.
