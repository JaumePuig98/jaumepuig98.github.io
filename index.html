About Me

I am a dedicated space biotechnology researcher with a background in biomedical engineering and passion for exploring life in extreme environments. I lead exciting projects on radiation tolerance and the human microbiome, teaming up with experts in astrophysics and gaining insights into the challenges of long-term space missions and human health, using microorganisms to enhance well-being on Earth and beyond.

I am a strong advocate for open science and contribute to projects such as NASA GeneLab and the iGEM Space Network, supporting global efforts in space-related synthetic biology and multiomics.

Beyond my research, I am dedicated to STEAM education and working with iGEM and Lecturers Without Borders to inspire future explorers. Through mentoring and lectures, I aim to make science exciting and accessible to students of all ages, connecting scientific concepts to practical applications.
