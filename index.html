About Me

I am a space biotechnology researcher with a foundation in biomedical engineering and a passion for improving life in extreme environments. My work focuses on pioneering research into radiation tolerance and the human microbiome, bridging astrophysics and synthetic biology to address the challenges of long-term space exploration while enhancing health on Earth. From laboratory experiments to international collaborations, I thrive at the intersection of innovation and discovery.

As a strong advocate for open science, I actively contribute to global initiatives like NASA GeneLab and the iGEM Space Network, driving advancements in space biology and multiomics. I believe that transparency and collaboration are key to unlocking the full potential of scientific progress for humanity's benefit.

Beyond research, I am dedicated to STEAM education, inspiring future generations through programs such as iGEM and AulaSPUTNIK. Through mentorships, lectures, and interactive workshops, I make complex scientific concepts engaging and accessible.
